\subsection*{Citation}%
%
The standard commands \verb|\textcite| and \verb|\parencite| are available for citations.
If the author's name should be used in the sentence, the former is suitable, e.g., \textcite[2-3]{Bayerlein_2018} refers to \textcite{Bayerlein_2016}.
If the citation should be placed in parentheses after the statement, the latter is recommended \parencite{Zaeh_2018_cirp}.
Grouped citations at the end of a sentence are written as follows: \parencite{Kleinwort_2018_ijat_2,Kleinwort_2018_pm,Kleinwort_2018_ijat_2}.
Citing online sources can be challenging since the author and publication year are not always available.
Comparing \textcite{Heuss_2018} and \textcite{iwb_Startseite}, it becomes clear that in the latter case, the page title is used instead of the standard schema.\par%
%
Standards are cited as \textcite{ISO_10218_2}.
Certain settings must be configured in the BibTeX export of the reference management software.
The document type should be book with the following entries:%
%
\begin{itemize}%
    \item Standard type and number as title%
    \item Full title as subtitle%
    \item Publisher as publisher%
    \item Year as date%
    \item author must not be assigned!%
\end{itemize}%

\subsection*{Abbreviations}%
%
Abbreviations can be defined in {./src/abbreviations.tex}. There are specific cases for expressions whose plural forms do not end with "s." In such cases, short and long forms of the expression must also be defined for the plural.\par%
%
\begin{itemize}%
    \item \verb|\gls{ros}| writes the full form on first occurrence in the document \gls{ros}, and the abbreviation from the second occurrence onward \gls{ros}% 
    \item \verb|\glspl{ap}| uses the plural in long form \glspl{ap} and then in short form \gls{ap}%
\end{itemize}%
